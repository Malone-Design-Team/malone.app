Week 1

This is the week of your last menstrual period, perhaps before you know you're pregnant. Your body is preparing for ovulation.

Details
Week 2

Conception typically takes place around this time, when your ovary releases an egg that is fertilized by sperm.

Details
Week 3

The fertilized egg forms a cluster of cells which move towards the uterus. Your hormone levels are changing, which may lead to mood swings.

Details
Week 4

The blastocyst implants in the uterine lining, where it will develop into the baby and placenta. You might notice fatigue and slight bleeding.

Details
Week 5

Your baby is tiny, but its heart, brain, and spinal cord are starting to form. You may experience morning sickness or mood swings.

Details
Week 6

Your baby’s facial features are developing. Hormone levels spike, resulting in nausea and heightened emotions.

Details
Week 7

The baby’s arms and legs begin to bud. You might feel slightly more tired and nauseous than usual.

Details
Week 8

Your baby is now an embryo with a heartbeat. Breast changes and backache can be discomforting.

Details
Week 9

Your baby’s most critical development period ends this week. You might notice weight gain and mood swings.

Details
Week 10

Fingers and toes are forming. You may notice changed food preferences or aversions.

Details
Week 11

Your baby’s genitalia are developing. Some symptoms may ease, leaving you feeling more energetic.

Details
Week 12

Your baby will start to move, but you won’t feel it yet. You may notice your waist thickening.

Details
Week 13

Your baby can make fists and facial expressions. Hormone levels are evening out, causing mood swings to decrease.

Details
Week 14

The baby’s features are becoming distinctly human. You might feel more energetic now.

Details
Week 15

You might be able to find out the sex of your baby during an ultrasound this week. Weight gain continues and you may start to "show."

Details
Week 16

Your baby now has reflexes. You might start to feel your baby move, and pregnancy "glow" is on full display.

Details
Week 17

Your baby can hear your voice. Your nipples might darken as your blood volume increases.

Details
Week 18

Your baby is developing fingerprints and toe prints. You will start to feel the baby move more noticeably.

Details
Week 19

Your baby may have hair on its head now. You may experience occasional abdominal aches.

Details
Week 20

The baby might begin to recognize your voice. The halfway point may bring excitement or anxiety.

Details
Week 21

Your baby's movements become more coordinated. Indigestion and heartburn may bother you.

Details
Week 22

The baby’s sight is developing. You may observe stretch marks.

Details
Week 23

Your baby is gaining weight. You may notice swelling in your ankles and feet.

Details
Week 24

The baby’s brain is growing rapidly. You may experience backaches and increased sex drive.

Details
Week 25

The baby’s wrinkles are filling out as fat forms. Trouble sleeping may be an issue due to your growing abdomen.

Details
Week 26

The baby’s lungs are developing. You may find yourself noticing your baby’s movements more frequently.

Details
Week 27

Your baby can open and close its eyes now. You might feel physically uncomfortable due to hormones and growth.

Details
Week 28

Your baby is moving frequently. Feeling the baby’s positions may become a new pastime.

Details
Week 29

Your baby's senses are developed. Mild swelling of feet and ankles might persist.

Details
Week 30

The baby is gaining more weight. You may experience shortness of breath and difficulty finding a comfortable sleeping position.

Details
Week 31

The baby may be moving a lot. Your breasts may be leaking colostrum, the precursor to breast milk.

Details
Week 32

Your baby has periods of sleep and wakefulness. Mood swings may return due to hormonal changes.

Details
Week 33

Your baby might be showing behaviors like sucking its thumb. You may experience indigestions and heartburn.

Details
Week 34

The baby’s immune system is developing. You may feel Braxton Hicks contractions more frequently.

Details
Week 35

The baby’s kidneys are fully developed. You may notice change in baby’s movements as it prepares for birth.

Details
Week 36

The baby is getting ready to face the world. You may start feeling pressure in the pelvis as your body prepares for labor.

Details
Week 37

Your baby’s organs are ready to function on their own. You may feel contractions intensifying.

Details
Week 38

The baby continues to build fat layers. You may feel more frequent urges to urinate as the baby presses on your bladder.

Details
Week 39

The baby’s chest is becoming stronger. Nesting instincts may lead to strong desire to prepare home and life for baby.

Details
Week 40

Your baby is considered full-term now. You may feel discomfort as you wait for labor to start. You might feel anxious and excited as your due date is near.
